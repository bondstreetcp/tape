Screening & backtesting

🔍 The Screener

Filter the universe by valuation, growth, margins, quality, technicals — or run a named strategy preset from the purple Strategy dropdown. Hover the i next to it for an in-app explanation of each. What they do:

✦ Magic Formula (Greenblatt)
Good businesses at cheap prices.

Ranks every name by earnings yield (cheapness) + return on capital (quality), sums the ranks, takes the best.

Net-Net / NCAV (Graham)
Deep value — a dollar of working capital for less than a dollar.

Trades below net current assets minus all liabilities. Very rare in large caps (usually 0 in the S&P 500) — try Broad 1500 or Russell 3000.

Piotroski F-Score (0–9)
A 9-point fundamental-strength checklist.

Profitability, cash flow, leverage, margins & efficiency trends. 7–9 = strengthening; pair with cheap stocks to avoid value traps.

Shareholder Yield (Faber)
All the cash a company returns to owners.

Dividends + net buybacks + net debt paydown — not just the dividend. Higher = more capital returned.

Open the screener →
Also try: the plain-English screener at the top — type something like "profitable companies under 20× earnings growing over 15%" and the AI builds the filter.

⏮️ Backtest

Test a strategy over ~5 years vs. the cap-weighted benchmark, with an equity curve and CAGR / max-drawdown / Sharpe. Two button rows: price strategies (momentum, trend, low-vol, equal-weight) and the factor screens above (hold the Magic/Net-Net/Piotroski/Shareholder-Yield basket).

Open the backtester →
Read the fine print: factor-screen backtests apply today's fundamentals across all of history (look-ahead bias) and use today's constituents (survivorship bias) — they're indicative, not a live track record. The UI flags this in red.

🤖 The AI analyst

Throughout the app, AI (Google Gemini, web-grounded) helps you read the data:

  • Ask AI — conversational Q&A on any company (Overview tab), with web sources.
  • Generate research note — a one-click buy-side memo (thesis, variant view, valuation scenarios, catalysts, risks).
  • Explain the move — why a stock moved over the window you've selected on the chart.
  • Head-to-head compare & earnings-call summaries — on the Peers and Filings tabs.

📝 For beta testers

A few things that would help us most:

  • Kick the tires on the screens & backtests — do the results look sensible? Anything obviously wrong?
  • Try odd tickers and the international universes — tell us where data is missing or looks off.
  • Push the AI — ask hard questions, generate research notes; flag anything inaccurate or generic.
  • Note anything slow, broken, or confusing — wording, layout, dead ends.

Known beta notes: data is a daily snapshot (intraday only for live quotes on watchlists); Yahoo serves ~4–5 years of annual financials; some international names carry less data; the AI needs a moment to think on big questions. Not investment advice — verify before relying on anything here.
